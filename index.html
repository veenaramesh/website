About

I'm Veena.

I am a Computer Science and Cognitive Science student from Northern Virginia, graduating in May 2021 from the University of Virginia.

Over the past three years at the University of Virginia's College of Arts and Sciences, I have absorbed myself in the intersections between computer science, philosophy, linguistics, neuroscience, and psychology. How does the mind work? And how can our understanding of our mind help us create more interesting and accurate models? I have sought after every opportunity to understand how I can operate within this intersection to find solutions to complicated problems.

I love to paint, draw, and read. Currently, I am reading Why Only Us, a book discussing the debate on the evoluton of language by Noam Chomsky, and Calling a Wolf a Wolf, a set of poems by Kaveh Akbar.
